Analyze the data to estimate the purchase price of gas at any date in the past and extrapolate it for one year into the future.

The price estimate for any date in the past between two points in the dataset will be linearly interpolated between the two points. The forecast for the following year will be based on the trend and seasonality of the historical data.

The trend is estimated by considering the 12-month moving average of the data, which is shown in the plot below.

In[]:

The trend is approximately linear, so a linear regression model combined with Fourier terms is used to estimate the trend and seasonality.

The optimal order of the Fourier series is determined by considering the RMSE of the model and the p-value of the Shapiro-Wilk test for the residuals.

In[]:
def find_best_model(data, max_order):
    validation_results = pd.DataFrame(columns=["p", "rmse"], index=range(1, max_order+1))
    for order in range(1, max_order+1):
        candidate_model = PriceEstimator(data, fourier_order=order)
        p, rmse = candidate_model.validate_model()
        validation_results.loc[order] = p, rmse
    
    return validation_results

The RMSE decreases as the order of the Fourier series increases, indicating that the model is overfitting the data. The p-value of the Shapiro-Wilk test also decreases, although not monotonically. This means that as the order of the Fourier series increases, the residuals are further from a normal distribution.

A trade-off between the two metrics is achieved by setting the order of the Fourier series to 6.

The price estimate for any date in the past is simply the value of the interpolated series at that date. No assumptions are made about the confidence interval of this estimate.

The price estimate for any date in the future is obtained by the fitted model. It is simplicistically assumed that the variance of the residuals is constant over time, therefore the 3-sigma confidence interval is determined by considering the residuals of the historical data.
